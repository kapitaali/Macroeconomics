# Chapter 37 — Climate Change and Macroeconomics: Sustainability and Green Growth

## 37.1 The DICE Integrated Assessment Model

Nordhaus (1992) links the Ramsey growth model to a climate system. Output net of climate damage:

$$Y_t^{net} = A_t K_t^\alpha L_t^{1-\alpha}\cdot\Omega(T_t)\cdot(1-\Lambda(\mu_t)),$$

where $\Omega(T) = 1/[1+\pi_1 T + \pi_2 T^2]$ is the damage function and $\Lambda(\mu) = b_1\mu^{b_2}$ the abatement cost. The social planner maximizes:

$$\max_{\{c_t,\mu_t\}}\int_0^\infty e^{-\rho t}\frac{c_t^{1-\eta}-1}{1-\eta}\,L_t\,\mathrm{d}t$$

subject to capital accumulation and the geophysical carbon-climate system.

---

## 37.2 The Social Cost of Carbon

$$SCC_t = -\mathbb{E}_t\int_t^\infty e^{-\int_t^s r(\tau)\,\mathrm{d}\tau}\frac{\partial Y_s^{net}}{\partial E_t}\,\mathrm{d}s.$$

Extreme discount rate sensitivity: Nordhaus ($\rho = 4.5\%$) obtains $SCC \approx \$37$/tCO$_2$; Stern ($\rho = 0.1\%$) obtains $\approx \$300$/tCO$_2$. The choice of $\rho$ is a normative judgment about intergenerational welfare weights, not a positive empirical question.

## 37.3 Climate-Macro Feedback and Green Transition

Physical climate risk (damage to capital from extreme weather) and transition risk (stranded fossil fuel assets under a rapid carbon tax) both affect aggregate investment and credit. Central banks and regulators are extending macroprudential frameworks to include climate stress tests and TCFD-aligned disclosure requirements.
