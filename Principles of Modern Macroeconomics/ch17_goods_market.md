# Chapter 17 — The Goods Market: Equilibrium and Adjustments

---

## 17.1 Equilibrium with Price Rigidity

In the short run output is demand-determined. Goods-market equilibrium:

$$Y = \mathcal{E}(Y, r, G, T, Y^f, e),$$

where $\mathcal{E}$ is aggregate expenditure, $Y^f$ foreign income, and $e$ the real exchange rate. Unplanned inventory investment closes the gap; the adjustment:

$$\dot{Y}_t = \phi[\mathcal{E}(Y_t) - Y_t], \quad \phi > 0,$$

converges to $Y^*$ globally when $\partial\mathcal{E}/\partial Y < 1$.

---

## 17.2 The New Keynesian IS Curve

Embedding household Euler equations into the goods market:

$$\hat{x}_t = \mathbb{E}_t[\hat{x}_{t+1}] - \sigma(i_t - \mathbb{E}_t[\pi_{t+1}] - r_t^n),$$

where $\hat{x}_t = Y_t - \bar{Y}_t$ is the output gap and $r_t^n$ the natural (Wicksellian) real rate. Solving forward:

$$\hat{x}_t = -\sigma\sum_{k=0}^\infty \mathbb{E}_t[i_{t+k} - \pi_{t+k+1} - r_{t+k}^n].$$

Current output is the discounted sum of all future real rate deviations from neutral — the long rate matters, not just today's short rate. This forward-looking structure is central to understanding forward guidance (Chapter 29).

---

## 17.3 Demand Complementarities and Multiple Equilibria

When aggregate desired expenditure $\mathcal{E}(Y; Y^e)$ is increasing in both $Y$ and the expected aggregate demand $Y^e$, and when $\partial\mathcal{E}/\partial Y + \partial\mathcal{E}/\partial Y^e > 1$, a high-output equilibrium and a low-output equilibrium can coexist — the animal-spirits mechanism of Chapter 15 in market equilibrium.
