# Chapter 22 — The Government Sector: Fiscal Policy and Public Debt

---

## 22.1 The Government Budget Constraint

The flow constraint in debt-to-GDP terms:

$$\dot{b}_t = (r_t - g_t)\,b_t - s_t,$$

where $b_t = B_t/(P_t Y_t)$ is the debt ratio and $s_t = (T_t - G_t)/Y_t$ the primary surplus. The **intertemporal government budget constraint**:

$$b_0 = \int_0^\infty e^{-\int_0^t (r_s - g_s)\,\mathrm{d}s}\, s_t\,\mathrm{d}t.$$

Sustainability requires that the PV of future primary surpluses equals the current debt stock.

---

## 22.2 Ricardian Equivalence

Barro (1974): under infinite horizons (or operative bequest motives), non-distortionary taxes, perfect capital markets, and rational expectations, the timing of taxes does not affect household consumption. A deficit-financed tax cut leaves the intertemporal budget constraint unchanged; private saving rises one-for-one with the deficit.

Ricardian equivalence fails with: liquidity constraints, distortionary taxes, OLG structure, or lower government borrowing costs than households. Empirically, the MPC out of tax rebates is positive but less than one (Johnson, Parker, and Souleles, 2006).

## 22.3 Fiscal Multipliers

In a New Keynesian model at the ELB, Christiano, Eichenbaum, and Rebelo (2011) show:

$$\mu_G^{ELB} = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{(1-\delta_G)\kappa\sigma}{(1-\beta\rho)(1-\rho)}} > 1,$$

where $\rho$ is the persistence of the ELB spell. The multiplier can substantially exceed one at the ELB because the central bank does not offset fiscal expansion with higher rates.

## 22.4 Debt Sustainability and Fiscal Rules

Sustainability condition: $s_t > (r_t - g_t)\,b_t$.

When $r < g$ (Blanchard, 2019), the cost of debt in terms of required surpluses is modest — but the risk of regime change ($r$ rising, $g$ falling) justifies precaution. Fiscal rules (Germany's debt brake, EU Stability and Growth Pact) impose binding constraints but their effectiveness depends on enforcement and political economy.

The IMF **fiscal gap**:

$$FG = (r^{adj} - g)\,b_0 - s_0 - \text{required adjustment.}$$

A positive $FG$ indicates the need for fiscal consolidation to meet the IGBC.
